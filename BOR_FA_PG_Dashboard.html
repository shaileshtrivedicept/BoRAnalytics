Representative remarks — positive
Studio HD4000 (MHD) — Housing Design Studio I
“The learning outcomes of the first semester in the Master of Housing Design are clearly mentioned, relevant and a good basis for the further programme. The exercises are broken down into modules with a logical sub-structure, ensuring progressive development from individual statement through analysis, design explorations and integration.”
Studio CR4002 (MCR) — Conservation Studio
“The programme was structured in the form of six modules with a logical progression from documentation to analysis and interpretation, leading to understanding of traditional building materials, structural assessment and exposure to key theoretical texts. This sequencing is commendable and reflects strong pedagogic thinking.”
Studio AT4000 (MAT) — Tectonics in Architectural Discourse
“A good quality of work was evident, with much experimentation and exploration. Students demonstrated genuine engagement with material and structural investigation, and the final outcomes showed strong evidence of the learning journey through the semester.”
Representative remarks — critical / constructive
Studio HT4003 (MAHR) — History and Theory Studio
“The problem is clearly stated but the methods to achieve the learning outcomes may need rethinking. What is in the brief reads more as studio outcomes than as learning outcomes per se. The studio encourages exploration, but the thematic framework may be too structured for the level of independent inquiry expected at PG.”
Studio AT4003 (MAT) — Architectural Technology Studio
“The studio brief lays out the ideological underpinning and sets general aspirations but does not get specific regarding methods or tools. There are multiple case studies and exercises included which do not find mention in the brief, making it difficult for a reviewer to evaluate whether stated learning outcomes are being met.”
Studio CR4000 (MCR) — Documentation and Conservation
“The experiment with combining manual and digital documentation is a welcome exercise. However, the sequencing of assignments appears to have been rushed in the second half, with insufficient time for students to consolidate their documentation learning before moving to assessment. The choice of buildings for study could have been more carefully curated.”
Representative remarks — negative
Studio HR4002 (MAHR) — Architectural History Studio
“The students’ work did not demonstrate achievement of the learning outcomes except at the most cursory level. Most exercises kept students at documentation rather than analysis and critical thinking — allowing learning at the lowest common denominator. Their exposure to primary sources was inadequate for a research-focused PG programme.”
Studio ID4020 (MID) — Interior Design Studio
“The brief does not clearly state the intent and tends to be vague about intended deliverables. There is excessive focus on macro-level understanding of the site and the context, with insufficient translation into interior-scale design decisions. The overall studio felt inconsistent, with the quality of outcomes varying widely across students.”
Key findings from sentiment analysis
01 — Positive tone dominates77.1% of FA PG reviews (236) are positive-toned — the highest positive share of any faculty reviewed. This reflects a structurally strong PG cohort with experienced panelists who engage constructively. Only 4.9% of reviews (15) are negative-toned.
02 — Brief alignment is the top concern12% of all remarks address brief alignment to outcomes (Q3) — the most commented criterion. The most common critique: studio briefs that are too vague or broad to allow clear evaluation of whether students have achieved the stated learning outcomes.
03 — General Comments carry substantive feedback199 of 306 reviews (65%) include a General Comment beyond Q1–Q11. These are the single most substantive source of qualitative feedback in the FA PG dataset and frequently contain the most pointed programme-level observations.
04 — P1 and P2 near-identical in languageP1 generated 123 positive reviews vs 113 for P2 — almost equal. This near-parity in both volume and tone is a distinctive feature of FA PG reviews compared to UG programmes, suggesting strong inter-panel calibration across all 6 programmes.
05 — MCR most negatively worded programmeMCR (Master of Conservation and Regeneration) has the highest negative-review share at 15% (6 of 40 reviews), driven by studio-level structural issues. “Limited” and “problem” are the most flagged negative terms, pointing to brief scope and conservation methodology concerns.
06 — Sentiment reliably predicts scoreReviews with positive language average +6.61; mixed +3.64; negative +0.40. The strong divergence between positive and mixed confirms that language tracks genuine studio performance — but the negative mean of +0.40 (not negative) suggests even critical reviews occur in broadly scoring-positive studios.
